Topics of interest

RISE-DL welcomes original research, prior work, preliminary results, work-in-progress, and exploratory research directions on scalable and efficient deep learning across heterogeneous computing environments—from IoT and edge devices to cloud infrastructures—spanning model design, training, inference, and deployment. Topics of interest include, but are not limited to:

  • Training for long-term sustainability

    Quantization-aware training, pruning-aware training, knowledge distillation, and green-aware neural architecture search.

  • Scalable training of generative AI

    KV-cache optimization for LLMs, memory-efficient attention algorithms, and parameter-efficient fine-tuning.

  • Fast inference and serving techniques

    Speculative decoding for LLMs, early-exit strategies, and caching mechanisms.

  • On-device, continual, and incremental learning

    Test-time adaptation, online and lifelong learning, and personalization at the edge under resource constraints.

  • Data-centric efficient deep learning

    Data pruning, compression, selection, and dataset distillation.

  • Benchmarking, metrics, and evaluation

    Methodologies for efficiency and sustainability, including energy consumption, latency, memory footprint, and carbon emissions.

  • Distributed learning across the edge–cloud continuum

    Federated learning, split learning, and gossip learning.

Submission guidelines

We invite submissions in two categories:

  • Short papers: from 3 to 4 pages, including figures, tables, and references.
  • Long papers: from 6 to 8 pages, including figures, tables, and references.

All submissions must be written in English, anonymized for double-blind review, and prepared using the ACM article template, as recommended in the IoT 2026 submission guidelines. At least one author of each accepted paper is required to register for the conference.
